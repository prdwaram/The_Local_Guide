# Hyderabad Local Context

## City Overview
Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, is known as the "City of Pearls" and "City of Nizams". It's a vibrant blend of historic culture and modern technology, home to major IT companies and rich Mughal heritage.

## Famous Street Food
- **Hyderabadi Biryani**: The crown jewel - aromatic basmati rice with marinated meat, cooked in dum style
- **Irani Chai**: Strong milky tea served with Osmania biscuits at iconic Irani cafes
- **Haleem**: Slow-cooked meat and wheat stew, especially popular during Ramadan
- **Lukhmi**: Savory pastry filled with spiced minced meat
- **Dosa and Idli**: South Indian breakfast staples available everywhere
- **Pani Puri (Golgappa)**: Crispy hollow puris filled with spicy tangy water
- **Mirchi Bajji**: Deep-fried chili fritters, a monsoon favorite
- **Double Ka Meetha**: Bread pudding dessert with saffron and dry fruits
- **Qubani Ka Meetha**: Apricot dessert, a Ramadan special

## Popular Food Areas
- **Charminar Area**: Paradise Biryani, Shadab, Pista House
- **Gachibowli**: Modern restaurants and cafes
- **Banjara Hills**: Upscale dining
- **Secunderabad**: Irani cafes like Cafe Niloufer

## Major Festivals
- **Bonalu**: Telangana festival honoring Goddess Mahakali (July-August)
- **Bathukamma**: Floral festival celebrating life and womanhood (September-October)
- **Ramadan & Eid**: Grand celebrations with special foods and markets
- **Ganesh Chaturthi**: 10-day festival with elaborate processions
- **Diwali**: Festival of lights celebrated with enthusiasm
- **Dussehra**: Traditional celebrations across the city

## Best Time to Visit
- **October to February**: Pleasant weather (15-30°C), ideal for sightseeing
- **Avoid**: April-June (very hot, 35-45°C)
- **Monsoon**: July-September (moderate rainfall, cooler but humid)

## Daily Habits & Culture
- **Language**: Telugu, Urdu, Hindi, and English widely spoken
- **Work Culture**: Major IT hub with global companies; traffic peaks 9-11 AM and 6-9 PM
- **Tea Time**: Evening chai culture is strong, people gather at cafes
- **Late Dinners**: Restaurants stay open late, dinner often after 8 PM
- **Weekend Spots**: Hussain Sagar Lake, Necklace Road, malls, and cafes

## Must-Visit Places
- **Charminar**: Iconic 16th-century monument
- **Golconda Fort**: Historic fort with sound and light show
- **Ramoji Film City**: World's largest film studio complex
- **Hussain Sagar Lake**: Heart-shaped lake with Buddha statue
- **Salar Jung Museum**: One of India's largest museums
- **Birla Mandir**: White marble temple with city views

## Transportation
- **Metro**: Efficient network connecting major areas
- **Auto-rickshaws**: Common but negotiate fare or use meter
- **Ola/Uber**: Widely available
- **TSRTC Buses**: Extensive public bus network

## Shopping
- **Laad Bazaar**: Famous for bangles near Charminar
- **Begum Bazaar**: Wholesale market
- **Inorbit, GVK, Forum**: Modern shopping malls
- **Shilparamam**: Arts and crafts village

## Local Etiquette
- Respect religious sites (dress modestly)
- Bargaining is common in local markets
- Remove shoes before entering temples and some restaurants
- Hyderabadis are generally warm and helpful to visitors
